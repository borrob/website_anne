Coming up!

It’s already been a great start to the season and November will only bring more exciting concerts!

An absolute highlight will be the performance of Stravinsky’s Le Sacre du Printemps with Daan Kortekaas (piano) and visuals by Marcel Weirckx. The visuals will synchronise with the music through an interactive computer system and will feature pre-recorded movements by dancers Kenzo Kusuda & Jenia Kasatkina inspired by original Nijinsky choreography. Music and visuals combined, this is bound to be a very special event! Part of the Young Pianists Festival in Amsterdam, it will take place on Sunday 22nd, 11am at the Bimhuis.

Straight after, I’ll be heading to Breda for the Flute Festival and will join Ilonka Kolthof in performing the wonderful works of Allan Segall, Bart Spaan and Ned McGowan from the Dutch Piccolo project.

The following Sunday (29th), Amsterdam’s Microfest is on! An entire day of microtonal music at the MGIJ. Ensemble Scala will at open the day (11am) with an array of new and old works for smaller and larger microtonal ensemble.

I'm also looking forward to joining the Intercontinental Ensemble in their DIY series, featuring the music of Wim Hendrickx at Splendor in Amsterdam, later that day (15h)!

Hope to see you somewhere here!
