Piano and technology

Technology is a huge part of our everyday lives thus it seems only fitting to incorporate it in one’s artistic expression. There is a beautiful (and ugly!) array of sounds to explore and music to make combining the two.

I became fascinated with the possibilities of electronic music and sound manipulation ever since I moved to the Netherlands in 2008. My Master’s thesis was on the art of performing on Piano with Live Electronics, focusing on the development of classical pianistic traditions and techniques and expression through gesture in this medium. More here

Similar but not the same, a key interest became Piano and Live Coding. The primary differences between the two are that here the coder(s) is as equal a performer as the pianist and the improvisatory nature of Live Coding often leads to less conventional means of notation, if at all. Currently, this is one of my key interests and I am active in a number of projects. More here
